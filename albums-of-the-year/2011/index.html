Albums of 2011

10
Mogwai
Hardcore Will Never Die, But You Will
Atmospheric instrumental rock.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "How to Be A Werewolf"
9
Tycho
Dive
Beautiful, intelligent electronic music with plenty of melody and soul.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Dive"
8
And So I Watch You From Afar
Gangs
Thrashing, fun, upbeat instrumental rock from Ireland's finest.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "7 Billion People All Alive At Once"
7
King Creosote & Jon Hopkins
Diamond Mine
Dreamy folk infused with electronica. Warming yet wistful. Jon Hopkins never fails.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Bubble"
6
Frank Turner
England Keep My Bones
English folk music about... er... England. Varies from upbeat rowdiness to yearning regret.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "If Ever I Stray"
5
...And You Will Know Us By The Trail of Dead
Tao of the Dead
Return to form for the Trail of Dead lads, with a more progressive rock style album.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Pure Radio Cosplay"
4
Youth Lagoon
The Year of Hibernation
Powerful, lump-in-the-throat inducing intimate dream pop.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "July"
3
M83
Hurry Up, We're Dreaming
Ridiculously epic electronica with plenty of anthemic qualities.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Reunion"
2
Bon Iver
Bon Iver, Bon Iver
Beautiful indie folk in a perfectly constructed album. Seeing these guys live was my highlight of the year.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Holocene"
1
The Antlers
Burst Apart
A fantastic follow-up to the amazingly heart-wrenching "Hospice" - whilst not topping said album (I'm not sure anything ever could), "Burst Apart" continues to show The Antlers' ability to write fantastic songs with intelligent poetic lyrics.
Listen on Spotify Watch on Youtube: "Putting the Dog to Sleep"
